WORK IN PROGRESS

Kuggen

A game to boost social skills and collaboration for autistic chilren

Bachelor's thesis in Computer Science and Engineering - Spring 2019

Project Report

GitHub Repo

About

Autism is a disorder that impacts an individual's life in different ways. For example, social skills and collaboration abilities are often affected negatively based on what demands society has on us today.

Kuggen is specifically and carefully designed for children with autism, as a part of a bigger project at Chalmers University of Technology. Our goal was to help the children practice their social and collaboration skills in early age, with the use of the game. To achieve this, we applied design guidelines for the specific target group, as well as game design theory to achieve a collaborating nature of the game.

The end result was a co-located multiplayer game, requiring four iPads. The game was implemented in Swift. Players must solve a puzzle by moving and aligning four cogwheels together. Achieving this will take the players to a new level, whereas each level increases in difficulty and requires more teamwork to be completed.

The project received the highest grade and during playtests it showed great potential to be expanded upon in the future. A thorough description of the entire project can be read in the report (Swedish).
