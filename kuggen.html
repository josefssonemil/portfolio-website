Why did we create this?

In today's life, there is an increasing demand of individual productivity. Simoultaneously, social media and our smartphones are designed to keep us using the apps. This can affect one's mental health in a negative way. It also exists numerous timer applications for our smartphones. The problem is that an individual is already focusing on the smartphone rather than the work or studies at that point.

Project Halo is designed to negate the need of a smartphone while studying or working. It is as a hybrid between a lamp and a timer and can be used for both purposes. The prototype consists of an Arduino in the bottom, connecting two softpots (pressure sensors) on the top of the circle, a LED-strip and a distance sensor aiming upwards in the circle.

To turn it on, you put the hand in the circle (distance sensor notices a change in distance). Then, using the pressure sensors, the user can "drag" the finger to set a timer. Each LED-light represents two minutes, and when releasing, the timer will start counting down. For every 2 minutes, a LED-light will be turned off, until all are turned off. A finished session is represented by a pulsating light to gain user attention.

Evaluation showed a positive user opinions and the idea could definitely be implemented further with proper access to materials. The entire project, from ideation to evaluation, can be read in the project report.
